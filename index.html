Pei Zhang

I am a Postdoctoral Fellow at the Biostatistics Branch (BB), Division of Cancer Epidemiology & Genetics (DCEG), National Cancer Institute (NCI), National Institutes of Health (NIH), mentored by Dr. Paul S. Albert and Dr. Jianxin Shi.

My research develops statistical and computational methods for longitudinal and correlated data, with applications to genetics, genomics, and cancer epidemiology. I focus on mixed modeling approaches for characterizing genetic effects and heritability in longitudinal phenotypes, with applications to prostate cancer risk stratification and early detection.

My work spans methodological statistics, statistical genetics, machine learning in epidemiology, and nutritional epidemiology. I have published in leading journals including Biostatistics, Annals of Applied Statistics, Advanced Science, and Journal of Nutrition.

I received my Ph.D. in Mathematical Statistics from the University of Maryland, College Park (2025), where I was recognized with the NIH Outstanding Graduate Research award.
